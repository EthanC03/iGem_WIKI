Future Experiment
Upregulation of Butyrate Synthesis
To ensure the feasibility of our project, we planned to prove the HBD and BCD proteins’ enhancement of butyrate synthesis by testing both rate-limiting steps.
We will first add a fixed amount of acetyl-CoA and NADH for the testing of HBD, and butyric acid, acetyl-CoA, FAD, and NAD+ for the testing of BCD, while varying the concentration of purified beta-hydroxybutyryl-CoA dehydrogenase and butyryl-CoA dehydrogenase respectively. Since the NADPH and NADH serves as the electron carrier (energy source) in the reactions, it would be formed along the reactions catalyzed by HBD protein (beta-hydroxybutyryl-CoA dehydrogenase) and BCD protein (butyryl-CoA dehydrogenase) respectively (Figure 2 and 3). We can, therefore, measure the rate of change of the concentration of NADPH and NADH with a spectrophotometer to determine the activity of the protein. For the results, we would expect the rate of change of NADPH and NADH concentration to increase along with the rise of the concentration of beta-hydroxybutyryl-CoA dehydrogenase and butyryl-CoA dehydrogenase. This would confirm that the proteins encoded by our DNA constructs, HBD and BCD, could successfully upregulate the production of butyrate.
Upregulation of Vitamin D Receptor
Expression
After confirming the increase of butyrate, we further planned to test the relationship between butyrate and the upregulation of Vitamin D receptor (VDR) expression. We plan to treat the HCT cell line, also known as the human colorectal carcinoma cell line. HCT cell lines are used because the microbiome in the colorectal cells absorbs butyrate.

The specific details of the experiment are included in the experiment page. If the result demonstrates that the VDR expression increases with the addition of butyrate in a time-and-dose dependent manner, it would confirm our hypothesis that our product, which enhances the synthesis of butyrate, could upregulate the VDR level and, therefore, increase the efficiency of Vitamin D binding activity.
